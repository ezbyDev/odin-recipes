How to make Pizza


A delicious pizza you can make in just 20 minutes!

This Brooklyn-style brick oven pizza is pure perfection—crispy, airy crust with just the right chew, rich tomato sauce, and a beautifully melted cheese topping that brings everything together. It tastes like it came straight from a classic New York pizzeria, with that irresistible balance of crunch and flavor.

The best part? You can make it yourself! I’ll show you exactly how to recreate this masterpiece at home, so you can enjoy authentic, restaurant-quality pizza anytime.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups warm water
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 2 ¼ tsp active dry yeast
  • 3 ½ cups bread flour
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup crushed San Marzano tomatoes
  • 1 tsp oregano
  • ½ tsp salt (for sauce)
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • 12 oz shredded mozzarella
  • ¼ cup grated Pecorino Romano
  • ¼ tsp red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Fresh basil (optional)

And after you gather all the ingredients, the last this is to just follow these steps.

Steps:

  1. In a bowl, mix warm water, sugar, and yeast. Let it sit until foamy (about 5-10 minutes).
  2. Add bread flour, olive oil, and salt. Knead until smooth and elastic (about 10 minutes).
  3. Place dough in a greased bowl, cover, and let it rise until doubled (about 1-2 hours).
  4. Preheat oven to the highest temperature (500°F or higher) with a pizza stone inside.
  5. In a small bowl, mix crushed tomatoes, oregano, salt, and black pepper.
  6. Punch down the dough and roll it out thin on a floured surface.
  7. Transfer dough to a pizza peel or baking sheet with parchment paper.
  8. Spread tomato sauce evenly, then add shredded mozzarella and Pecorino Romano.
  9. Bake on the hot pizza stone for 7-10 minutes, until crust is crispy and cheese is bubbly.
  10. Remove from oven, top with red pepper flakes and fresh basil if desired, and enjoy!
